All the Places You Should Not Fly

Generally speaking, avoid tiny airports.

Even if you have a private jet.

According to the Bureau of Transportation Statistics, out of the United States' 335 airports, 12 reported average departing flight delays of over half an hour. The dataset includes all flights between January and August of 2018.

If you're planning on using MMH to get back from your ski vacation in Mammoth, CA, beware: departing flights are delayed an average of 50 minutes. Let's watch how cool it is that this map refuses to zoom in anywhere besides the Pacific Ocean.

Barnstable Municipal Airport in Hyannis, MA is a regional airport on Cape Cod. The airport reported 85 flights this year, with an average delay time of 38 minutes.

According to the airport's website, "Youngstown-Warren Regional Airport is the perfect departure point for your next trip." However, this small airport in Vienna, OH leaves departing travelers stranded, on average, for over an hour on departing flights.

With only two departing flights recorded in 2018, it's debatable whether this can truly be considered an airport, but if you choose to fly out of YGA, expect an average delay time of one hour and three minutes.

International airports aren't all bad

Flights out of the largest airports in the country tend to experience relatively timely departures, with the exception of the Pacific Northwest (ironic, considering that region's proclivity toward birds).

Generally speaking, airports east of the Mississippi are also more timely than their western counterparts--perhaps relaxed attitudes prevail as proximity from New York City decreases.
